The California coastline stretches over 800 miles, and every inlet tells a story. For those of us who worked the waters, the fog wasn't an obstacle — it was an ally.

## The Geography of Opportunity

The coast between San Diego and Santa Barbara is riddled with hidden coves, rocky outcroppings, and stretches of beach that see no visitors after dark. Each one was a potential landing point.

### Hidden Coves

Some spots were so well-concealed that even the Coast Guard didn't bother patrolling them. We knew every rock, every current, every tide schedule.

### Fog as Cover

The marine layer that rolls in most evenings wasn't just weather — it was our partner. Visibility dropping to fifty feet meant freedom of movement.

## The Network

Nobody works alone in this business. From the boat captains to the shore crews to the drivers who moved product inland, it was a carefully orchestrated chain.

### Trust and Paranoia

The irony of smuggling is that your survival depends on trusting people you can never fully trust. One weak link and the whole operation collapses.

## The Beginning of the End

Every run felt like it could be the last. Eventually, that feeling stopped being paranoia and became prophecy.
