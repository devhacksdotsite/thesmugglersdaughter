If someone told me in 1990 that I'd eventually earn a college degree, hold a steady career, and write a book, I would have laughed.

## The Myth of the Clean Break

Movies show redemption as a single moment — the decision to change. Reality is hundreds of small decisions, made daily, often in the face of temptation to fall back into old patterns.

### Old Habits

The thinking patterns that made smuggling feel normal don't disappear overnight. Cutting corners, bending rules, justifying bad choices — these are deeply ingrained.

### The World Doesn't Forget

Even when you've changed, the world remembers who you were. Job applications, background checks, the looks from people who know your history.

## Building Something New

Starting over at 35 with a criminal record and no legitimate work history is exactly as hard as it sounds.

### Small Victories

The first legitimate paycheck. The first semester completed. The first time someone trusted you with responsibility. These moments build on each other.

## The Long Road

There's no finish line for redemption. It's a daily practice, not a destination. Some days are harder than others, but the distance from who I was grows with every right choice.
