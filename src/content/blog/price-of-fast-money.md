Everyone in the game thinks they'll be the one who gets out clean. Nobody does. The money comes fast and disappears faster.

## The Illusion of Wealth

Cash in hand feels like freedom. But when you can't deposit it, can't explain it, and can't stop looking over your shoulder, it becomes a prison of its own.

### Living Beyond Your Means

The cars, the boats, the lifestyle — all of it was a house of cards. One wrong move and everything vanishes overnight.

### The People You Attract

Money draws people to you. Fast money draws the wrong people. Figuring out who's genuine and who's looking for an angle becomes a full-time job.

## Relationships Destroyed

The secrecy required to maintain a criminal life is corrosive. You can't be honest with anyone — not your family, not your friends, not yourself.

### The Lies Compound

One lie requires ten more to support it. Before long, you've built an entire fictional life that you have to maintain 24 hours a day.

## The Reckoning

Prison wasn't just a consequence — in some ways, it was a relief. The exhaustion of maintaining a double life finally ended, even if what replaced it was far worse.

### What I Lost

Time. That's the real currency. Years I can never get back with people who moved on without me.
